# Vocabulary - In The Future: Killers Dispose of Bodies By Sending Them Back in Time

## Core Topic Vocabulary

### Crime & Punishment

| Word | Part of Speech | Meaning | Example from Text |
|------|---------------|---------|-------------------|
| **hitman** | n. | a person paid to kill someone | "Joe worked as a specialized hitman known as a looper" |
| **executioner** | n. | a person who carries out a death sentence | "...recruited several young men to act as executioners" |
| **incinerator** | n. | a furnace for burning waste | "...take the body to an incinerator to dispose of all evidence" |
| **syndicate** | n. | an organized group of criminals | "the five largest criminal syndicates in the future" |
| **torture** | v./n. | inflict severe pain on someone | "his younger version was being tortured by the organization" |
| **captors** | n. | people who capture someone | "managed to fight back against his captors" |

### Time & Temporal Concepts

| Word | Part of Speech | Meaning | Example from Text |
|------|---------------|---------|-------------------|
| **destined** | adj. | meant to happen in the future | "destined to be shot by their younger self" |
| **inevitable** | adj. | certain to happen | "it was only a matter of time before Joe would face the same fate" |
| **cease** | v. | to stop or come to an end | "he would cease to exist" |
| **vanish** | v. | disappear suddenly | "making the victims disappear forever" |
| **legacy** | n. | something handed down from the past | "the future Rain Maker" |
| **predestination** | n. | the belief that events are predetermined | implied throughout the narrative |

## Idioms & Collocations

| Expression | Meaning | Context |
|------------|---------|---------|
| **close the loop** | to complete a cycle, especially eliminating witnesses | killing one's future self |
| **face to face** | in person, meeting directly | "first time they sat face to face" |
| **track down** | to find someone after searching | "older Joe had tracked them down" |
| **come to pass** | to happen | (biblical/elegant form of "occur") |
| **break the cycle** | to end a repeating pattern | "to break this endless loop" |
| **connect the dots** | to understand the relationship between things | "finally connected the dots" |
| **cease to exist** | to stop being | "would immediately cease to exist" |
| **ripple through** | to spread through | "actions in the present ripple through time" |
| **hunt down** | to search for to capture/kill | "was hunting down every retired looper" |
| **wipe out** | to eliminate completely | implied in destroying organization |
